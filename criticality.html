Testing Self-Organized Criticality Across the Main Sequence using Stellar Flares from TESS

Adina Feinstein, Darryl Seligman, Maximilian Gunther, & Fred Adams

arXiv:2109.07011

TL;DR

What is Self-Organized Criticality?

Flares from TESS

To test if all stellar coronae are in self-organized critical states, we need a large sample of stellar flares across a variety of spectral types. For this, we used all of the stars observed at high cadence by the Transiting Exoplanet Survey Satellite (TESS). This sample has 161,836 stars that are located all across the HR diagram (see left), with the majority of stars falling along the main sequence.

Because we had such a large number of stars, each observed for at least 25 days, we needed a scalable approach to identifying and characterizing flares in the light curves. For this we used the stella convolutional neural networks (CNNs; Feinstein et al. 2020).

Inferring Properties about Stellar Coronae

We break our sample up into 6 bins: 5 based on the stellar mass on the main sequence (purple through green) and 1 for stars on the red giant branch (yellow).
