Who Am I?

Hi! My name is Adina Feinstein. I am currently a senior undergraduate at Tufts University and will be graduating this May (2018) with a B.S. in Astrophysics and a minor in English. As a member of the Tufts Department of Physics and Astronomy, I have conducted research under the guidance of Professor Danilo Marchesini on high redshift galaxies and, after my junior year, I worked as an intern at NASA Goddard Space Flight Center under the guidance of Dr. Joshua Schlieder. There, I analyzed IR spectra of low-mass stars to classify them by spectral type and also to identify if they looked young. Since then, I have been roped into the Kepler/K2 community and have been a contributing author on a few exoplanet detection papers.

In my spare time, I love baking, crocheting, and playing board games.
