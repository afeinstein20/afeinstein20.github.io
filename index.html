About Me


Adina Feinstein

Graduate Student

Hello and welcome to my webpage! I recently graduated from Tufts University with a BS in Astrophysics. Outside of academics, I may have an addiction to baking, and love crocheting, writing short stories, and sitting out by Lake Michigan. This fall, I will be pursuing a Masters degree in Physics at the University of Chicago and am so excited to start doing research in the Astronomy & Astrophysics Department!

Previously, at Tufts, my work focused on calculating gravitational lensing factors and associated errors for sources located in the Hubble Frontier Fields campaign. My contribution to this collaboration is publicly available and can be accessed through this link to a really cool class="w3-text-blue" href="http://cosmos.phy.tufts.edu/~danilo/HFF/HFFexplorer.html">interactive tool one of our members built!

Research Interests

Broadly, I am interested in observational exoplanet and stellar astrophysics. In the future, I hope to focus more on planet formation around low-mass stars, with an insterest in binary systems.

Current Projects

Currently, at the University of Chicago, I am developing a set of Python tools for exploring the Transiting Exoplanet Survey Satellite (TESS) Full-Frame Images (FFIs). This package will not only create lightcurves for all sources in the TESS Input Catalog for Tmag < 16 that will be hosted on the MAST servers, but will also be able to create custom lightcurves for sources in Gaia. Users will be able to create custom target pixel files, lightcurves, and useful figures and videos for visualzing their data, such as the example below. You can follow my progress on GitHub!

Outreach

I am involved in several forms of outreach, mostly focusing around working with younger children. Upon landing in Chicago, I started volunteering at the Hyde Park Neighborhood Club (HPNC) after camp speciality club called the Maker Club/Animation Art, which focuses on combining science and arts and crafts. I am also a new volunteer for the Letters to a Pre-Scientist and Skype a Scientist programs.
